May 1 — First Night in Jakarta

2025-05-01

The flight from Reykjavik feels like it lasted a lifetime, and stepping out of the airport is like walking into a warm, wet wall of air. After the pale stillness of Iceland, Jakarta hits every sense at once — the heat, the noise, the smell of exhaust and frangipani mingling in the dark.

My guesthouse in Menteng is small and unpretentious, exactly what I need. I drop my bag and head straight out, hunger overriding exhaustion. A street stall just around the corner is still busy, the wok firing and hissing under the glow of a single bulb. The nasi goreng arrives fast — fragrant, a little smoky, deeply satisfying. I eat standing up, jet-lagged and grinning.

Sleep, though, refuses to come. The heat presses down on the sheets like a second blanket, and the city never quite goes quiet. Motorcycles weave through the night, voices carry from somewhere nearby, a distant call drifts across the rooftops. I lie still and let it all wash over me. This is the sound of a city that doesn't pause for anyone — certainly not for a tired traveller from the far north.

I'm exhausted. I'm thrilled. Jakarta is already something else entirely.
