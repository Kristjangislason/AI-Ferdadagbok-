May 3 — Drifting Toward the Orangutans

2025-05-03

The klotok moves at its own unhurried pace, and I'm learning to move with it. There's something immediately humbling about trading solid ground for this wooden riverboat, letting the Sekonyer carry us deeper into Tanjung Puting's green heart.

Yanto spots them before I do — three orangutans on the riverbank, half-hidden in the tangle of roots and hanging leaves. I follow his pointing finger and catch my breath. A mother sits impossibly still, watching us with calm, ancient eyes, her baby pressed tight against her chest, tiny fingers clutching rust-colored fur. The boat slows. Nobody speaks. The only sounds are the engine's low murmur and the river lapping at the hull.

We stay as long as we dare, then drift on.

By evening the jungle has swallowed the last of the light. I climb to the roof deck with a bowl of instant noodles — simple, perfect — and eat with my legs dangling over the edge as the darkness fills in around us. Then, one by one, the fireflies appear. First a handful, then dozens, threading silently between the trees along both banks like scattered sparks refusing to go out.

I sit there long after the noodles are gone, not quite ready to go below.
