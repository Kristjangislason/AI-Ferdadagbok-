May 2 — First Night in Jakarta

2025-05-02

The flight from Reykjavik feels like it happened in another lifetime, yet here I am, stepping out into Jakarta's thick, humid embrace. The heat hits immediately — a wall of warm air so different from Iceland's cool silence that my body doesn't quite know what to make of it. I'm exhausted down to my bones, but underneath the fatigue something fizzes: excitement, or maybe just disbelief that I'm actually here.

My guesthouse in Menteng is small and unpretentious, exactly what I need. I drop my bags and follow my nose straight to a nearby street stall, where a plate of nasi goreng appears in front of me like a small miracle — fragrant, smoky, deeply satisfying. I eat standing up, watching the street hum with life around me.

Sleep, though, refuses to come. The heat presses down on the sheets like a second blanket I never asked for. Outside, Jakarta stays wide awake — motorbikes, distant calls, the murmur of a city that never quite settles. I lie there listening, too tired to move, too wired to rest, staring at the ceiling fan as it stirs the warm air around without really cooling anything.

There's something strangely wonderful about it. I'm not comfortable, not yet — but I'm here.
