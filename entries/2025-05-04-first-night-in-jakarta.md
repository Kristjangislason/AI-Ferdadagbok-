# May 4 — First Night in Jakarta

The heat hits me the moment I step out of the terminal — thick, wet air I'm almost drinking rather than breathing. Jakarta doesn't ease you in gently.

My taxi crawls through traffic that never seems to sleep. Motorcycles weave between cars like schools of fish, restless and purposeful, slipping through gaps I wouldn't have thought possible. I watch it all through the window, already a little stunned.

My hotel is in the Menteng district. After a quick shower I drift back out into the night, hungry and curious. On a small street corner I find a man with a wide, knowing grin and a smoking wok. I order nasi goreng, and he watches with quiet amusement as I reach, confidently, for the chili.

I immediately regret it. Tears stream down my face — from the heat of the peppers, yes, but also from something harder to name. That particular feeling of being genuinely, completely far from home. Not lost, exactly. Just... very far.

My first evening in Southeast Asia, and it's already made me cry. I take another bite anyway.
