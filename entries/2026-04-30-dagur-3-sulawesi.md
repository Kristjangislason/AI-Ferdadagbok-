# Dagur 3 - Sulawesi

For the text you wrote in Polarsteps, your main options are:

1. **Manual copy/paste**
Best if you only have a few entries. Copy each Polarsteps story into pages or blog posts on your website.

1. **Export your Polarsteps data**
Polarsteps says you can download a copy of your data, and third-party examples indicate the text entries are available inside exported JSON files such as `trip.json`. You could then convert those entries into website posts.

1. **Use WordPress plugin, if your site is WordPress**
There is a third-party Polarsteps WordPress plugin that imports steps and can generate blog posts from Polarsteps text and images. Polarsteps explicitly says it is **not officially supported**, so it could break later.

1. **Custom integration**
A developer can parse your Polarsteps export and turn the stories into pages/cards/blog posts on your site. This is the most flexible and reliable option if you want the text to look native on your website.

So: **you can embed the trip map officially, but connecting/syncing the written text usually requires export, copy/paste, WordPress plugin, or custom coding.**
