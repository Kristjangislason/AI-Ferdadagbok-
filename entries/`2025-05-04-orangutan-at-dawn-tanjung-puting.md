# May 4 — Orangutan at Dawn, Tanjung Puting

The river feels different at dawn. I wake to a world half-dissolved in mist, the water and the treeline bleeding into one another in shades of grey and green. The klotok sits quietly in the current, and for a moment I don't move — just breathe it in.

Then the rangers spot him. Tom. A massive male orangutan, moving through the canopy with that slow, deliberate authority that only comes from decades of knowing exactly who you are. His flanks are enormous, his face wide and ancient-looking. We watch in near silence as he makes his way through the branches, utterly indifferent to our presence below. I feel genuinely small in the best possible way.

Later, the afternoon heat wins. I climb off the back of the boat and drop into the river — and the water is *unbelievably* clear. After days of travelling, it feels like a small miracle. I float on my back and watch the jungle ceiling drift past overhead, listening to the sounds coming in from both banks.

Two days on this river, and I'm already starting to wonder how I'll ever feel at home anywhere with walls.
