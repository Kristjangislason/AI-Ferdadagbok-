# May 3rd — First Night in Jakarta

The flight from Reykjavik feels like it happened in another lifetime, yet here I am — stepping out into Jakarta's thick, heavy air and feeling the heat wrap around me like a second skin. After the clean cold of Iceland, it's a shock to the system, but an electric one.

I find my guesthouse in Menteng, small and unpretentious, and drop my bags with the relief of someone who has been carrying the world on their shoulders. The neighborhood hums around me before I've even caught my breath.

Hunger wins over exhaustion. A nearby street stall is serving nasi goreng, and I order without hesitation. The first bite is smoky, savory, and deeply satisfying — exactly what twelve thousand miles of travel deserves at the end of it. I eat standing up, watching motorbikes thread through the evening traffic like water finding its way downhill.

Back in my room, sleep refuses to come. The heat presses in from every angle, and the city outside is very much alive — horns, voices, the distant pulse of music, a rooster somewhere that has entirely lost track of time. I lie still and just *listen*, too tired to be frustrated, too awake to drift off.

Jakarta is already making sure I know it's here.
