# May 1 — First Night in Jakarta

The flight from Reykjavik was endless. By the time I stepped off the plane in Jakarta, my body had forgotten what timezone it belonged to. But the moment the doors slid open and that wall of heat hit me — thick, humid, alive — exhaustion gave way to something else entirely. I was here.

I found a small guesthouse in Menteng, dropped my bag, and went straight back out. The city pulled me in. At a street stall just around the corner I had my first nasi goreng — the wok hissing, the smell of garlic and kecap manis rising in the warm air. It was simple and perfect.

Sleep didn't come easy that night. The room was hot despite the fan, and Jakarta never quiets down. Motorbikes, voices, the distant thrum of a city of eleven million still very much awake. I lay there listening, sweat on my skin, grinning in the dark. Five weeks of this ahead of me.
